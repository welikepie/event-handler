Our Checklist

A checklist containing the things we think are necessary to ensure you give a great talk.

Have something to add to this list? Email us!

Things to consider when preparing for your talk

  • When talking in an unfamiliar city - check the journey from the station/airport to the venue beforehand
  • If your nerves become too much - Know where the bathrooms are
  • What are you wearing? - check that your clothes aren't seethrough under bright lights
  • Need an internet connection for your presentation? - Check that there is one!
  • If you're presenting from a high stage - Consider the length of your skirt/kilt
  • Check the size of screen you'll be presenting on - in both pixels and inches/metres
  • Doing a long talk? - take a beverage with you on stage
  • Know who your audience is
  • Don't want your talk recorded/tweeted? - Tell the organisers before hand
  • Require Pyrotechnics, Animals, Robots or Cyborg Dogs with Rocket Launchers? - Check with the organisers!
  • When taking questions from the audience - Repeat the questions asked back to the audience. This can be useful for both those who did not hear the question and also if the event is being recorded
  • Get familiar with the venue - know where you will be, where your notes will be and where your slides will be displayed
  • Have a dry run of your presentation - Get used to plugging into a second display and being ready to talk. Get the timing right. It is a good idea to do this with a small audience who can offer you critical feedback, such as colleagues over a lunch break
  • If you're doing an evening talk or staying late for the after-party - Check how you will get home in the evening! Your journey may not be the same as it was in the day time
  • Check your travel plans on the day - there may be things preventing you (like engineering works) from taking your previously planned route
  • Depending on the content of your talk - you may want to check with the organisers if they have any policies on content they deem problematic
  • Back-up your talk - on USB or to a service like Dropbox, in case you have any problems with your laptop on the day
  • When making your slides with custom fonts/media - make sure these are embedded within the slidedeck. If you're using someone elses set-up to display your presentation, they may not have these things on hand.
  • Don't be afraid to ask questions - If you're unsure about something or just have a request, ask the organisers! They're there to help.
